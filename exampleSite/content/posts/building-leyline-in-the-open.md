Most themes I've used arrived as finished artifacts. Polished, pre-decided. This time I wanted to do it differently: think out loud, pick at the trade-offs, and keep the small commits that don't usually make it past someone's local branch.

The shape of Leyline reflects that. The sticky header didn't need three iterations to land; the second one was right. But the home page took a while: featured posts vs. latest posts vs. a third "evergreen" section that I tried and then quietly deleted. You don't see that deletion in the diff. You see the result.

If you're building a theme too, the best advice I can give is to write a couple of real posts in it. Markdown showcases lie. A real post — with its uneven structure, its links, its tag list — surfaces exactly the rough edges abstract test content hides.
